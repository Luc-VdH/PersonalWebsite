First Year

Introduction to Computer Programming:

Introductory programing using Python, covering the fundimentals of programming such as boolean logic, string manipulation, input and output, and functions

Introduction to Object Oriented Programming:

Object oriented programming in Java, using classes, abstract classes, interfaces and simple GUIs.

General Physics:

A basic understanding of physics past the high-school level, covering kinematics, fluid dynamics, electricity, magnetism, relativity and more.

Pure Mathematics 1:

Covered mathematical concepts including set theory, functions, trigonometry, proof methods, limits, calculus, vectors, complex numbers and matrices.

Modeling and Applied Computing:

Intro to mathematical models and their applications in computer systems using MatLab and Octave, covering topics such as estimation theory, dimensional analysis, probability and differential equations.

Dynamics:

Mathematics applied to dynamical systems, covering vector calculus, complex analysis, the Kepler problem and Maxwell's equations.

Second Year

Computer Science 2:

Advanced programming techniques for efficiency such as data structures and parallel programming, as well as elational database design, graph theory, computer architecture, and mobile development and design.

Introduction to Electrical and Electronical Engineering:

Study of electricity including circuit analysis, magnetic circuits and electricity distribution. Study of electronics including passive and active circuits, semiconductors (diodes and transistors), computer logic and basic digital memory.

Introductory Statistics:

Study of statistical concepts including probability, random variables, probability distributions and hypothesis testing.

Systems and Business Analysis:

Basic analysis of businesses to assess the need for an information system. Used UML modeling to complete Business Case and User Requirement Specification Documents.
